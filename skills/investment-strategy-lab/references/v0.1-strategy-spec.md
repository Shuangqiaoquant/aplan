# v0.1 Strategy Specification

This is the first explicit baseline. It is intentionally conservative and designed to be falsified.

## Status

`v0.1-research`

No live trading. No paper trading until APlan validation artifacts justify promotion and the user approves.

## Primary objective

Find A 股 candidates with evidence of durable relative strength and acceptable downside control, then test whether those candidates outperform a chosen benchmark after realistic execution costs.

Initial benchmark: 沪深300 unless a strategy-specific benchmark is documented.

## Current lesson from local validation

Existing APlan reports show that earlier fixed-cycle strategies failed robustness checks:

- 40-day strategy was sensitive to rebalance date.
- Multi-horizon validation selected 20 days in training but failed on validation.

Therefore v0.1 must not assume fixed rebalance timing is reliable. It should emphasize robustness, regime separation, and explicit invalidation.

Additional local factor-lab result:

- Initial single-factor tests through `aplan-factor-lab` found that plain `momentum20`, `momentum60`, `turnover_trend20`, and `low_vol20` did not show a positive future-20-day top-minus-bottom spread in the current local sample.
- These factors may still be useful as ingredients or risk descriptors, but they should not be promoted as standalone buy rules without regime/industry conditioning and further validation.

## Candidate universe

Default universe:

- A 股 common shares available in APlan data.
- Exclude suspended, delisted, ST-like risk where data supports it.
- Exclude candidates with insufficient recent data or unreliable execution assumptions.

Prefer candidates with:

- adequate liquidity;
- clean recent data;
- no immediate execution block from limit-up/limit-down assumptions;
- comparable industry/benchmark context.

## Signal family

v0.1 is a research blend, not yet a validated strategy:

1. Relative strength vs benchmark.
2. Trend quality and drawdown control.
3. Volume/liquidity confirmation.
4. Fundamental or announcement support when available.
5. Valuation only as a risk modifier, not a standalone buy reason.

Avoid:

- pure low-valuation bargain hunting;
- pure rebound-after-crash logic;
- single-news-event buying without price/volume confirmation;
- indicator stacking that cannot be explained.

## Research scoring model

Use the scorecard in `decision-playbook.md`:

```text
relative_strength: 0-30
trend_drawdown:    0-20
liquidity_flow:    0-15
quality_catalyst:  0-15
valuation_risk:    0-10
execution_fit:     0-10
```

The current local quantitative bridge is:

- momentum rank feeds relative strength;
- low-volatility rank feeds trend/drawdown;
- turnover trend feeds liquidity/flow;
- daily_basic PE/PB can feed valuation_risk when valuation snapshots are supplied;
- announcement title-risk rules can cap or downgrade candidates when announcement events are supplied;
- announcement full-text analysis can strengthen evidence explanations but not positive score yet;
- fundamental snapshots can be displayed and risk-flagged when `period_end` and `publish_time` are supplied;
- fundamental risk downgrades are enabled conservatively: one visible risk caps a candidate at `research_candidate`, multiple visible risks cap it at `watch_only`;
- fundamental quality scoring, positive announcement catalysts, and richer execution checks must be explicit or marked missing.

This keeps v0.1 compatible with APlan's existing `score`, `confidence`, and `target_weight` fields while leaving room for better evidence later.

## Buy research trigger

A stock can become a buy candidate only when all are documented:

- why this stock is stronger or improving relative to benchmark/peers;
- what confirms participation or demand;
- what the expected holding horizon is;
- what invalidates the thesis;
- where the position size would be if paper trading were allowed;
- what data could prove the setup is false.

While status is `research_only`, position is always 0%.

The detailed buy-entry baseline is documented in `buy-entry-v0.1.md`.
In short: `research_candidate` means observe only; only `paper_candidate_if_validated`
or stronger candidates may enter next-day paper-buy confirmation, and only after
all eligibility gates pass.

## Next-day entry confirmation

A signal-day candidate is not automatically a buy, even for paper trading. The next trading day must confirm execution quality:

- no suspension or limit-down execution block;
- opening gap is not above the chase limit, currently 5% over signal-day close;
- close does not break below the signal-day low;
- breakout-continuation candidates must remain above the signal-day close;
- breakout-continuation candidates with weak next-day turnover should be downgraded or watched.

With daily bars only, this rule is most reliable for post-close paper-trade validation and retrospective review. True open-time confirmation requires intraday or real-time data.

## Sell / reject trigger

Reject or remove a candidate when:

- thesis evidence disappears;
- relative strength breaks materially;
- liquidity or execution risk rises;
- announcement/fundamental evidence contradicts the thesis;
- portfolio constraints would force excessive concentration;
- better candidates dominate the same risk bucket.

Selling is an exit discipline, not a prediction that price must fall.

Current exit review also checks:

- initial stop loss from cost basis, default 8%;
- deep loss risk escalation above 20%;
- trailing stop after a profitable move, default activation +10% and drawdown -12%;
- time stop when a position has not produced enough return after the planned holding window.

## Position template

For research discussion:

```text
research_only: 0%
paper_trade_candidate: 1%-3% paper weight
validated_candidate: 3%-7% target weight
max single stock: project risk policy, currently 10%
```

Sizing should shrink when volatility is high, invalidation is far away, or liquidity is weak.

## Metrics to track

At minimum:

- benchmark-relative return;
- max drawdown;
- turnover;
- hit rate;
- payoff ratio;
- average win / average loss;
- number of comparable signals;
- performance by year/regime;
- performance by rebalance offset or signal-date bucket;
- slippage/cost sensitivity.

## Promotion criteria

Research to paper requires:

- validation outperformance not concentrated in one offset, year, or regime;
- realistic costs/slippage;
- no obvious future data leakage;
- enough sample size for the horizon;
- evidence-layer variants must show adequate historical coverage before being promoted to rules;
- explicit user approval.

Paper to live consideration requires:

- documented paper trades;
- drawdown within policy;
- rule-following discipline;
- comparison with benchmark and rejected candidates;
- explicit user approval.

## Known weaknesses to investigate

- Whether A 股 data quality supports reliable event/fundamental timing.
- Whether relative strength works better by industry-neutral ranking.
- Whether fixed holding periods should be replaced by thesis-based exits.
- Whether market regime filters help or overfit.
- Whether simple factor ingredients only work after market-regime and industry-relative conditioning.
- Whether liquidity and limit-up/limit-down constraints erase apparent edge.
